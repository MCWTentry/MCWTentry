Technology

1960s 2020
TV While colored TVs were invented, they were very expensive. Most middle-class houses had black-and-white TV. Now, most families have colored TVs. It's a commodity to middle-class living.
Internet In the 1960s, internet was only an idea. It had began to develop, but it would be decades later before it would be considered important. Now, the internet is a vast system that connects people from across the world. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the internet has become the main way we connect with one another.
Phones In the 1960s, there were phones, but not like the ones we have today. They were dial phones attached to the wall or a table, meaning all they could do is call, and, you couldn't put it in your pocket. Nowadays, phones aren't just used for calling, but for several other things. Phones nowdays can almost do anything, and they are a big part of our lives.
Computers In the 1960s, computers were huge. The average people didn't have a computer. They were mainly used by big companies and the government. Nowadays, computers are tiny. So tiny, that they can fit in an average backpack. Many people own computers, and they are used for personal things (for example, me making this website).
Digital Cameras Digital cameras weren't around in the 1960s. Digital cameras are heavily used today.
GPS System GPS was not invented at this time. To navigate, people used maps, which were not as convinient. Nowadays, GPS systems are incredibly complex. You can put in directions for most anywhere and are given the quickest route to get there. It's magic.
Transportation In the new decade, people wanted smaller cars, so cars like the Ford Falcon, Chevrolet Corvair and the Plymouth Valiant. Now, cars have so many more features. Some cars can connect to the internet, and self-driving cars are not far away.
